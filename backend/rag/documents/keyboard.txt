class: keyboard
aliases: computer keyboard, mechanical keyboard, wireless keyboard, typing keyboard
category: electronics

description:
A computer keyboard is an input peripheral used to type text and issue commands to a computer. It contains alphanumeric keys, function keys, modifier keys (Shift, Ctrl, Alt), and navigation keys arranged in a standardized layout (most commonly QWERTY).

physical appearance:
Keyboards are flat, rectangular devices wider than they are tall. Standard full-size keyboards are approximately 44 cm wide and 14 cm deep. Compact and tenkeyless versions are shorter. They range from slim, low-profile membrane keyboards (about 1.5 cm tall) to taller mechanical keyboards (3–4 cm). Colors vary widely — black, white, gray, and RGB-backlit variants are common. Keys are arranged in staggered rows.

common use contexts:
- Desktop computer setups as the primary text input device
- Extended laptop use when the built-in keyboard is insufficient
- Gaming setups where mechanical keyboards are preferred for tactile response
- Office workstations, writing desks, coding environments
- Home office setups paired with a monitor and mouse

scene inference:
A standalone keyboard (without a visible laptop directly underneath) strongly suggests a desktop computer setup. If paired with a monitor and mouse, it confirms a full desktop workstation. A keyboard alone on a clean desk may indicate a writing or programming focus. Mechanical keyboards with RGB lighting often indicate a gaming or enthusiast computing context.

associated objects:
monitor, mouse, laptop, USB cable, desk mat, speaker, computer

typical locations:
desk, table, workstation

functional notes:
The keyboard's presence without a laptop suggests a desktop PC is nearby (even if not visible). It is rarely used in isolation — its context is almost always a broader computing workstation.
