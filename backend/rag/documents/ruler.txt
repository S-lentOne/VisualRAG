class: ruler
aliases: ruler, straightedge, measuring ruler, scale ruler, drafting ruler, set square
category: stationery

description:
A ruler is a flat, rigid measuring and drawing tool marked with a scale in centimeters, inches, or both. It is used to measure lengths, draw straight lines, and as a general reference tool in study, technical drawing, and crafts.

physical appearance:
Standard rulers are flat, rectangular strips 30 cm (12 inches) long and 3–4 cm wide, made from plastic, wood, or metal. They are typically transparent or translucent plastic, yellow wood, or stainless steel. The surface bears printed measurement markings. Shorter 15 cm rulers fit in pencil cases. Set squares are right-triangle-shaped rulers at 45° or 30/60° angles, used in geometry and technical drawing. Longer drafting rulers may be 45–60 cm.

common use contexts:
- Drawing straight lines in notes, diagrams, charts, and technical drawings
- Measuring lengths of objects or drawn elements in exercises
- Geometry and mathematics for constructing shapes, angles, and figures
- Technical drawing, architecture, and engineering work
- Cutting guides in craft and art projects
- Underlining text neatly in notes
