class: backpack
aliases: bag, rucksack, school bag, laptop bag, daypack, satchel
category: personal item

description:
A backpack is a bag carried on the back using two shoulder straps, used to transport personal belongings, books, electronics, and daily essentials. It is the primary carrying accessory for students, commuters, and travelers.

physical appearance:
Backpacks are rectangular or contoured bags ranging from 20 to 50 liters in volume. They stand 40–55 cm tall and 25–35 cm wide when full. Most have a main compartment, a front zip pocket, and side mesh pockets for bottles. Straps are padded for comfort. Common materials include nylon, polyester, and canvas. Colors vary widely — black, gray, navy, and olive are common for everyday use; bright colors for children. Laptop backpacks have a dedicated padded sleeve for a laptop. Some backpacks have external USB charging ports connected to an internal power bank pocket.

common use contexts:
- Daily carry for students: transporting books, notebooks, a laptop, and personal items to class
- Commuter bag: carrying work essentials and a laptop to and from an office or co-working space
- Travel daypack: carrying essentials during city trips or outdoor activities
- Gym bag: carrying workout clothes and gear
- Storing equipment: cameras, electronics, and accessories for field work

scene inference:
A backpack on or near a desk strongly suggests the scene is at a study or work location outside the home — a library, university, coffee shop, or office. A backpack placed on the floor beside a chair confirms someone is settled in for an extended session. An open backpack with contents spilling out (notebooks, chargers, bottles) gives rich context about the type of user and session. A packed backpack near a door suggests the person is preparing to leave. At home, a dropped backpack in a workspace suggests the person just arrived from school or work.

associated objects:
laptop, notebook, pen, phone, earbuds, water bottle, keys, wallet, charger

typical locations:
on a chair, on the floor beside a desk, hung on the back of a chair, on a table surface (if small)

functional notes:
The backpack is one of the strongest indicators of a student or commuter scene. Its contents (when partially visible or implied by context) narrate the nature of the session. A well-organized backpack with compartments visible suggests a methodical, prepared user. A bulging, disorganized one suggests a busier, more chaotic lifestyle or session.
