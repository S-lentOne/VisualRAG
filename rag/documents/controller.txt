class: controller
aliases: game controller, gamepad, joystick, PS5 controller, Xbox controller, Nintendo controller
category: electronics

description:
A game controller is an input device designed for interacting with video games. It translates physical inputs — button presses, thumbstick movements, trigger pulls — into in-game actions. Controllers are used with gaming consoles and increasingly with PC gaming.

physical appearance:
Modern controllers (PlayStation, Xbox) are ergonomic, symmetrical devices roughly 15 cm wide and 10 cm tall, designed to be held with both hands. They feature two analog thumbsticks, a directional pad (D-pad), four face buttons (typically labeled with symbols or letters), two shoulder buttons per side, and two analog triggers. They are wireless (Bluetooth) with a USB charging port. Colors vary: black, white, and console-specific color variants. Nintendo Switch Joy-Cons are smaller, modular controllers that can be attached to the Switch console or used independently.

common use contexts:
- Console gaming (PlayStation, Xbox, Nintendo Switch)
- PC gaming as an alternative to keyboard and mouse, especially for action and sports games
- Retro gaming with emulators on a computer
- Casual couch gaming in a living room setup
- Competitive gaming and esports training

A controller alone on a couch or living room setting suggests console gaming in a dedicated leisure context. Multiple controllers indicate multiplayer gaming. A controller near a laptop suggests casual gaming during breaks or off-work hours. A controller sitting prominently on a desk alongside study materials is a common student desk scene.
