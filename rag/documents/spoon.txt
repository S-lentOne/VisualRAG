class: spoon
aliases: tablespoon, teaspoon, dessert spoon, soup spoon, stirring spoon, cutlery
category: food and drink

description:
A spoon is a utensil consisting of a shallow oval or round bowl on the end of a handle, used for eating, stirring, and serving food and drink. In personal environments it most commonly appears during meals, snack breaks, or alongside hot beverages at a desk.

physical appearance:
Spoons range from small teaspoons (14–15 cm) to larger tablespoons and soup spoons (18–20 cm). They are typically made of stainless steel with a polished or matte finish, though plastic and wooden spoons also exist. The bowl (the oval end) is concave and smooth. Stainless steel spoons are silver-colored; plastic spoons may be white, black, or clear. A teaspoon may be left in a mug after stirring, a tablespoon left in a bowl or on a plate after eating.

common use contexts:
- Eating cereal, yogurt, soup, desserts, or snack foods at a desk or table
- Stirring coffee or tea in a mug
- Scooping sugar or instant coffee powder into a hot drink
- Eating a quick meal at a workstation between tasks
- Part of a full cutlery set at a meal setting

scene inference:
A spoon at a desk or workspace indicates a meal or snack break is occurring or has recently occurred. A spoon resting in a mug suggests hot drink preparation — coffee or tea with sugar stirred in. A spoon beside a bowl confirms eating behavior. A spoon on a desk alongside a laptop and other work materials suggests the person is eating while working, which is a common behavior during busy study or work sessions. A dirty spoon left on a desk surface hints at a casual, informal, or rushed eating moment rather than a sit-down meal.

associated objects:
cup, bowl (implied), laptop, notebook, can, bottle

typical locations:
inside a mug, on a plate or bowl, on the desk surface beside food or drink, on a napkin

functional notes:
The spoon is a transitional object in a workspace — it signals a food or drink moment occurring within what is primarily a work or study scene. Its presence adds a human, informal touch to the scene. A spoon in a mug specifically is a very common desk detail during working-hour beverage preparation.
