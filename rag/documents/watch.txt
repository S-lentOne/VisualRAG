class: watch
aliases: wristwatch, smartwatch, analog watch, digital watch, Apple Watch, timepiece
category: personal item

description:
A watch is a timekeeping device worn on the wrist. Traditional analog watches display time with hour and minute hands; digital watches show time numerically. Smartwatches add notification mirroring, fitness tracking, and app support on top of timekeeping.

physical appearance:
Watches consist of a case (the body housing the mechanism or display), a dial or screen, and a strap or bracelet. Cases are circular, square, or rectangular, typically 38–46 mm in diameter. Straps can be leather, rubber/silicone, metal link, or fabric (NATO strap). Smartwatches (Apple Watch, Galaxy Watch) are square or rounded-square with a digital touch display. Traditional analog watches have a glass face, metal case, and visible hands. Thickness ranges from 8 mm (slim dress watches) to 14 mm (sport and dive watches).

common use contexts:
- Daily timekeeping as a wrist accessory
- Worn during work or study to monitor time without checking a phone
- Smartwatches used to receive notifications, track exercise, and monitor health metrics
- Fashion accessory that complements an outfit
- Placed on a desk or nightstand when charging (smartwatch) or when the user prefers not to wear it while typing

scene inference:
A watch on a desk (off the wrist) indicates the user has removed it — common during typing-intensive work, sleeping, or exercising. A smartwatch on its charging puck or dock indicates charging behavior. A watch alongside wallet and keys forms the classic pocket/wrist dump of a person settling in. A smartwatch on a desk near a laptop suggests a tech-forward user who integrates digital and wearable devices into their workflow. An analog watch alone on a desk can be a deliberate display item if it is a high-quality timepiece.

associated objects:
phone, wallet, keys, laptop, charger

typical locations:
on the wrist, desk surface, nightstand, charging dock, inside a bag

functional notes:
A smartwatch versus a traditional watch on a desk can subtly indicate the tech-savviness of the user. Smartwatches require charging (so a charging cable or dock nearby is expected), while analog watches do not. The watch, especially when alongside keys and a wallet, is a reliable "person is present" indicator.
